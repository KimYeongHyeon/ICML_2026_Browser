ICML 2026 Poster Instructions

 

Author Registration

  • Authors of main conference and workshop papers have access to a limited number of reserved registrations that will be allocated on a first come first served basis
  • If you are not able to register then verify that your main conference or workshop accepted paper is listen in your My Stuff page
    • If you do not see your conference paper then make sure that you are logged in to the correct account. See this FAQ
    • If you have workshop paper the orgainizers of the worshop must first add your paper to their workshop
    • If you see your paper and are still not able to register then submit a support ticket and include the email that you are logging as and the name of your paper
  • See the author registration requiremnts in this FAQ

Poster

  • Visit your My Stuff page to see your papers.
  • If you do not see all your papers see this FAQ 
  • Changes you make to your paper on OpenReview will not appear on icml.cc until after we manually pull the changes 

Author Names

  • On icml.cc we use the names that authors have set in their profile. Authors can change the name that is displayed by editing their profile 

Video Recording

  • A video recording is recommended as a backup, but not required
  • You can find your SlidesLive Recording Link on your MyStuff page under Presentation History 
  • Workshops may use YouTube if you would like a pre-record

Poster Metadata

Authors of a paper will see [ Event Metadata for Authors (e.g. Poster and Slide Uploads...) ] on their poster page

  • We recommend that you upload the following optional metadata at least one week before the conference starts.
  • You can updated this content anytime you want
     
  • Poster Upload
    • Upload your virtual poster and thumbnail
    • Highly recommended
  • Topic
    • Pick the topic that best fits your paper
    • This is used to assign your poster a position in the poster session
    • Required
  • Slide Deck
    • This is the for oral presentations to upload a PDF slide desk to accompany their oral presentation.
    • See this wiki for details
    • Recommended for posters selected for a oral, optional for others
  • Project Page
    • The URL of a Github or other project page
    • Optional

 

Poster Session Changes

  • If you want to change your assigned poster session see this page for instructions on how to request a change.

 

Poster Printing

  • You can use any service you want to print your poster.
     
  • ICML is proud to partner with The Foster poster printing company.
    • The order deadline is June 15, 2026
    • Please place your order here: https://www.forceposter.com/order/order.asp?searchStr=icml
    • Be sure to include your poster number and date of presentation in the order form.
    • Confirm your file is accurate before you upload.
    • All authors need to pick up their poster the day they are presenting.
    • Authors can pick up their poster with their confirmation email at the poster pick up area near Hall B2 (East Gate) at COEX.
    • Poster pick up hours are as follows:
      • Tuesday, July 7, 9:00 - 18:00
      • Wednesday, July 8th, 9:00 - 18:00
      • Thursday, July 9th, 9:00 - 18:00
      • Friday, July 10th, 7:30 - 12:30
      • Saturday, July 11th, 7:30 -  7:30 - 12:30

 

Poster Sizes

Main Conference suggested sizes:

  • 36in (H) x 48in (W) or 91 cm (H) x 121 cm (W)
  • 36in (H) x 60in (W) or 91cm(H) x 150cm (W)
  • 36in (H) x 72in (W)  

Workshop

  • Must not exceed 36in (H) x 24in (W) or 91cm (H) x 61cm (W)   
  • Notice: Workshop posters must be in portrait format

 

Spotlight and Oral Posters

  • Spotlight posters and Orals will be indicated on poster boards via "SPOTLIGHT" or "ORAL" signs, which will be provided along with tape in the session rooms
  • Authors of spotlight posters and orals can pick up their spotlight signs as they enter the poster room

 

Poster Display Instructions

Main Conference

  • Use only the approved tape to attach poster to poster boards.  The approved tape will be provided by the conference
  • Only use the boards provided in the poster session. Do not hang your poster anywhere else in the venue
  • Do not use adhesive backed self-sticking posters or anything that can damage poster boards

Workshops

  • It is prohibited to hang posters or any signage on the walls in the workshop rooms or public spaces at COEX, Seoul, South Korea
  • All workshop poster sessions will be held in Hall A 
  • Workshop organizers will let you know where to hang your posters
  • Only the approved tape provided by the conference is permitted to hang your poster on the boards
  • Do not use adhesive backed self-sticking posters or anything that can damage the walls or poster boards
